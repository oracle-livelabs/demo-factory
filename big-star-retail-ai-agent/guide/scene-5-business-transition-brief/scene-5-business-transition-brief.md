# Lab 5: Scene 5 Business Transition Brief

## Introduction

This scene connects technical quality to business readiness. Instead of looking at one response, you evaluate whether the operation is ready for scale.

Estimated Time: 10 minutes

### Objectives

In this lab, you will:
- Load the transition brief in Scene 5.
- Review the readiness metrics shown in the dashboard.
- Translate those metrics into an operational narrative for stakeholders.

## Task 1: Load the Transition Brief

1. Open **Scene 5: Business Transition Brief**.
2. Click **Load Transition Brief**.
3. Review the summary text and the metric cards.

Expected result:
- A transition narrative appears between response quality and operations.
- Metric cards show repeat-case risk, verified memory, policy coverage, and pending reviews.
- The screen provides a business checkpoint before deeper controls.

## Task 2: Interpret What the Metrics Mean

1. Compare the four metric values as a group, not in isolation.
2. Focus on how verified memory and policy coverage influence decision consistency.
3. Note how pending reviews affect execution speed and oversight.

Expected result:
- You can explain why a good answer is not enough without governance capacity.
- You can identify where risk sits in the operating model.
- You can summarize readiness in language suitable for management review.

## Task 3: Why this matters?

The transition brief is where a promising pilot becomes an operational decision. Strong answers in isolated cases do not automatically mean the organization is ready to scale, because readiness depends on queue behavior, memory quality, and policy coverage moving together. Oracle AI Database 26ai's converged architecture supports that view by keeping those signals on a single trusted data foundation, giving customer AI-agent programs a clearer control point for quality, risk, and throughput as they scale.
